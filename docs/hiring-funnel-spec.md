# Halevora Hiring Funnel: Teardown of educate.io and Recreation Spec

Date: 2026-07-05. Sources: full crawls of educate.io, educate.io/hiring, all 7 job adverts,
and all 6 reachable application funnels on hiring.educate.io (raw HTML plus each Tally form's
embedded JSON definition). Raw artifacts sit in the session scratchpad (`hiring.html`,
`home.html`, `next_data.json`, per-form HTML).

This document is the spec. Implementation plans (task-by-task, TDD) follow per subsystem
once the stack decision in section 4 is confirmed.

---

## 1. What educate.io actually built

Two layers, two tools, one funnel.

| Layer | Domain | Platform | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand + job adverts | educate.io | Framer (single site, CMS collection for jobs) | Trust building and role selling |
| Application funnel | hiring.educate.io | Tally.so PRO on a custom domain | Qualification and filtering |

Key facts:

- The careers page (`/hiring`) is bare on purpose: an "OPEN ROLES" eyebrow, seven linked
  rows with `Remote` and `Full Time` pill badges, a footer. No hero, no culture pitch.
  The persuasion lives on the homepage (founder, mission, philanthropy) and on each job page.
- Job pages are Framer CMS items sharing one template (route `TsBYmTnm4`, slug variable).
- Apply buttons link to per-role Tally forms served on `hiring.educate.io`. The subdomain
  root returns 404: you can only enter a funnel through a job page, so every applicant
  arrives pre-framed by the sales copy.
- No job page shows compensation, benefits, or perks. The only money question in the whole
  system asks the applicant for their own current monthly gross income in USD.
- Tracking: RightMessage personalization on the job pages, with survey answers POSTed to a
  Make.com webhook. Tally captures hidden fields (UTM/source) and auto-detects respondent
  country to cross-check the stated one.

### 1.1 Job advert template (identical across all 7 ads)

1. Nav (Home, Our Mission, Career) + "Back" link
2. H1 role title + meta strip: Date Posted / Team / Location / Type
3. **Job Summary** (one paragraph, mission-loaded)
4. **Your Role** (eyebrow sub-label + 1-2 paragraphs)
5. **Ideal Candidate** (4-12 bullets)
6. **Requirements** (3-5 titled cards: bold lead-in + tagline + paragraph)
7. **What You'll Do** (numbered `001`-`006` in mono font, includes fixed daily call times)
8. **You Shouldn't Apply If** (3-4 blunt bullets: "You want a normal 9-5. This role is NOT
   easy & requires an all-in mentality.")
9. CTA band: "Think you're a fit? Apply Today." + APPLY NOW button
10. Legal footer (entity, privacy, terms)

The "You Shouldn't Apply If" section and the missing salary are deliberate: they filter
before the form ever loads.

### 1.2 Funnel anatomy (Tally, per-role)

Shared skeleton across all forms, one question per page, progress bar on, save-for-later on:

1. **Intro page** with a scarcity + effort frame: "we receive thousands of applications...
   please answer thoroughly & thoughtfully." Button: Apply Now.
2. **Identity block**: first name, last name, phone, email, Instagram handle (optional).
3. **Country** (text) + hidden auto-detected respondent country.
4. **Referral gate**: "Did someone on the team refer you?" Yes branches to "Who referred you?"
5. **Age** (number). Branch logic: under 16 jumps silently to a dead-end thank-you page;
   16-25 gets a "are you enrolled in school or university?" question (Yes also dead-ends);
   over 25 skips it.
6. **Phase interstitial**: "There are two phases to this application process:
   ✅ Personal Information ☑️ Professional Fitness." Manufactured sunk-cost midpoint.
7. **Role-specific screening** (see matrix below).
8. **Income anchor**: "What are you currently earning in monthly gross income?" (USD, number).
9. **Commitment gate** (some roles): "I understand this is a full-time role" / "I Agree".
10. **Two endings**: a warm one for completed paths and a shorter, vaguer one for
    disqualified paths. Rejection never says no.

Per-role matrix:

| Role | Form pages | Distinctive questions |
|---|---|---|
| Customer Support Rep | 17 | 5 service-scenario essays; "How long have you been following Iman?" |
| Short-Form Editor | 20 | Device, computer specs, internet speed (wifiman.com), best video link, brands worked with, travel frequency, full-time commit choice |
| Creative Designer | 16 | Design specialties multi-select, software multi-select, portfolio link with auto-DQ warning about sharing permissions, internet speed |
| Senior Copywriter | 33 | Seven 1-10 self-rating scales (ads, emails, VSLs...), revenue generated by copy, nights/weekends/holidays attitude, "what do you know about us", assignment promised at next stage |
| Full-Stack Developer | 19 | GitHub link up front, 4 stack essays (front-end langs, frameworks, back-end, methodologies) |
| Vlog Editor | 20 | Editing software, motion graphics, color grading (s-log), "most important part of an edit", GDrive work samples, I Agree gate |
| South African Talent | 21 | Department picker (6 + "not sure"), load-shedding contingency essay, internet stability, income anchor |

### 1.3 Design system (extracted)

- **Theme**: black `#000000` body via design tokens, near-black purple `#0c0012`, card
  surface `rgb(22,22,26)`, translucent white overlays `#ffffff1a`.
- **Accents**: blue family (`#166fee`, `#185cd3`, `#84caff`), green `#02b991`,
  form accent purple `#7957FF`, form focus underline amber `#FFB433`.
- **Type**: Hanken Grotesk (400-800) for everything, Fragment Mono for eyebrows, badges,
  and the `001`-`006` numbering. Text on forms `#DFDFDE`.
- **Buttons**: white pill, black text, uppercase labels ("APPLY NOW", "LEARN ABOUT US").
- **Forms**: Tally custom CSS explicitly written to imitate Typeform: borderless inputs,
  bottom border only, amber focus underline, fade-up entry animation, one question per view.
- **Motion**: smooth scroll module, appear animations. No video embeds anywhere in the
  hiring flow.
- The dark Framer site and the dark Tally form read as one product. That handoff continuity
  is the design achievement worth copying.

### 1.4 Funnel psychology worth stealing

1. Friction as filter: 17-33 pages and 5-10 required essays make low-intent applicants quit.
2. Silent disqualification: branch logic routes minors and students to a polite dead end.
3. Income anchoring: they learn each applicant's price before ever discussing pay.
4. Brand-affinity screen: "How long have you been following Iman?" measures culture buy-in.
5. Referral fast-lane: referred candidates get flagged at intake.
6. Commitment devices: progress bar, name piping ("Nice to meet you, {first name}"),
   phase banner, explicit "I Agree" full-time gate.
7. Practical-reality screens: internet speed, hardware specs, load shedding. Cheap to ask,
   priceless for remote hiring.

### 1.5 Their defects (do not copy)

- **Brand fracture**: every form is branded Consulting.com while the ads say Educate. One
  internal field still reads "Educate team". Applicants hit a different company at the
  moment of highest commitment.
- **CDN roulette**: repeated fetches served the wrong role's cached page or the wrong form
  entirely (Full-Stack URL returned the Copywriter form once).
- **Boilerplate bleed**: the Full-Stack summary says "Front-End Developer"; the Vlog Editor
  ad lists "excellent debugging and performance optimization skills".
- **Generic SEO**: every page reuses the homepage meta description.
- **404 subdomain root**: fine as a funnel choice, sloppy as an experience if anyone trims
  the URL.

## 2. The South African Talent play (their strongest asset)

The SA ad is a talent-pool page, not a single role. Its structure:

1. **Performance narrative**: "our South African team members have been some of our highest
   performers." Talent quality framing only. Cost, timezone, and English never appear.
2. **Future stake**: "we're now exploring the idea of opening an office in South Africa...
   you could be part of its foundation."
3. **Local proof**: "we opened a school in Cape Town" ties philanthropy to the market.
4. **Six department buckets** with example titles (Sales, Support, Marketing, Content &
   Creative, Product & Tech, Operations) plus "apply anyway" for everyone else.
5. **Reality screening in the form**: load-shedding contingency plan, internet stability,
   current income in USD.

Why this works: it flatters the market, promises a future the company never commits to,
collects a cross-department talent pool from one page, and quietly solves the two real SA
remote-work risks (power and connectivity) inside the form.

### Halevora's version

Adopt the structure, replace the trust assets with true ones. We cannot claim schools or an
office we do not have. Honest equivalents that keep the same slots:

- Performance narrative: cite our actual experience with SA team members if we have it, or
  frame it as a deliberate expansion ("we are building our next team hub around South
  African talent") rather than fake history.
- Future stake: a real commitment we can keep, like guaranteed ZAR-stable payout schedule,
  growth path to lead roles, or equipment/UPS stipends. A load-shedding stipend is a
  stronger promise than a hypothetical office.
- Local proof: none yet. Leave the slot empty rather than invent it, or fill it later with
  a real initiative.
- Department buckets: map to Halevora's org (see 3.2).
- Form: keep load shedding, internet, and backup-power questions verbatim in spirit. Add
  POPIA consent (South Africa's data law) to the intro page.

## 4. Stack decision

**Option A: parity stack (Framer + Tally PRO + custom domain).**
Ship in days. Framer ~$25/mo, Tally PRO ~$29/mo. Zero code to maintain. Limits: SaaS
lock-in, no custom scoring, applicant data lives in Tally, brand-consistency depends on
CSS hacks like theirs, and we cannot fix their class of CDN/routing bugs because we do not
own the layer.

**Option B (recommended): custom build in our stack.**
One Next.js app (same family as halevora-tasks: Next.js + Prisma + Supabase/Postgres),
two route groups (`/hiring`, `/apply`), deployed once. We own the funnel engine, the data,
the scoring, and the design system. The funnel engine is config-driven JSON (section 6),
so adding a role is a content change, not a code change. Admin review UI can reuse
halevora-monitor's viewer-login pattern later.

Recommendation: Option B. "Into our system" was the brief, we build product in-house, and
the funnel engine is reusable for every future Halevora brand. Option A remains the
fallback if we need applications flowing this week while B is built.

This repo (halevora-monitor) is the wrong home for the build. Create
`C:/Projects/halevora-hiring` as a fresh repo; this spec moves with it.

## 5. Copy playbook (tone rules)

- Blunt filters stay: a "You Shouldn't Apply If" section on every ad, an all-in mentality
  line, no salary on the page.
- Scarcity + effort frame on every funnel intro: many applicants, answer thoroughly.
- Two-phase framing with the checkmark banner at the midpoint.
- Name piping in at least two later questions.
- Two endings, warm and vague, so rejection never says no.
- Brand-affinity question adapted to Halevora (e.g. "How long have you followed our
  creators/brand?") only where it makes sense; drop it for developer roles.
- Every page gets a role-specific meta title and description (fixing their generic SEO).
- All copy passes the stop-slop rules already used in this repo.

## 6. Funnel engine spec (Option B)

**Form definition**: one JSON document per role, stored in Postgres, versioned.

Block types needed (complete list observed across all 6 forms):
`intro`, `text`, `textarea`, `email`, `phone`, `number`, `url`, `single_choice`,
`multi_choice`, `linear_scale` (1-10), `interstitial`, `legal_gate` (I Agree),
`ending` (multiple, addressable). Field flags: required, placeholder, help text.

**Logic**: rules attached to pages: `jump_if` (answer comparison → target page id),
`skip_if`, default next. Must support the observed patterns: age gates, referral branch,
choice-based DQ, answer piping (`{first_name}`, `{age}`).

**Runtime features**:
- One question per view, fade-up entry, progress bar, keyboard submit.
- Autosave per block to a server-side draft (their save-for-later), resumable by link.
- Hidden field capture: UTM params, referrer, and geo-detected country alongside the
  stated country.
- Silent DQ: terminal pages are indistinguishable in tone from success pages.
- Anti-spam: honeypot field + rate limit per IP; no CAPTCHA (friction budget is spent
  on the questions).

**Admin**:
- Applications table with per-role filters, DQ-path flag, referral flag, income answer,
  and country mismatch flag (stated vs detected).
- Webhook out (Make.com compatible) on submission, mirroring their pipeline.
- CSV export. POPIA/GDPR delete-by-email.

**Design tokens** (own palette, their contrast model): black base, one light accent for
focus states, white pill buttons, Hanken Grotesk + a mono accent face (or our existing
brand faces if defined), CSS variables only, matching this repo's convention.

## 7. Build phases

1. **Phase 0, content**: write all 7 ads + 7 form definitions (JSON) following 1.1/1.2.
   No code dependency; can start immediately.
2. **Phase 1, careers site**: listing page + job template + legal pages. Static, fast, SEO
   fixed per role.
3. **Phase 2, funnel engine**: schema, renderer, logic runner, autosave, endings.
   Unit-test the logic runner hard (branching is the safety-critical part).
4. **Phase 3, capture + admin**: submissions, hidden fields, webhook, admin table, export.
5. **Phase 4, polish**: motion, save-for-later links, analytics events per page for
   drop-off measurement (their stack cannot see per-question drop-off; ours will).

Each phase gets its own task-by-task implementation plan (writing-plans format) in the new
repo before code starts.

## 8. Compliance and ops notes

- POPIA applies to SA applicants: consent line on intro page, stated retention period,
  delete-on-request. GDPR equivalents for EU applicants.
- Income questions are legal but sensitive: mark optional-but-encouraged for SA (POPIA
  minimality principle) and store encrypted.
- Age question: we replicate the under-16 DQ (also a legal necessity for full-time work).
- Single Tally-style workspace bugs (wrong form served) do not exist in Option B; in
  Option A, mitigate by using one Tally workspace per brand, never shared.
